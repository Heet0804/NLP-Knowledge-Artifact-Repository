1. Tokenization

Definition: Tokenization is the process of breaking text into smaller units called tokens, such as words, subwords, or characters.

Purpose: It prepares raw Gujarati text for NLP models by converting sentences into processable units for further analysis.

Working Principle: The input sentence is divided into meaningful tokens using whitespace, punctuation, or subword tokenization algorithms. These tokens serve as the basic input for NLP models.

Example: "હું આજે કોલેજ જઈશ" → ["હું", "આજે", "કોલેજ", "જઈશ"]

Advantages:

  • Simplifies text processing.
  • Reduces sentence complexity.
  • Supports efficient language modeling.
  • Provides structured input for autocomplete systems.

Limitations:

  • Incorrect tokenization may reduce prediction accuracy.
  • Complex languages require advanced tokenization techniques.
  • Different tokenization methods may produce different outputs.

Applications:

  • Gujarati Autocomplete & Predictive Text
  • Machine Translation
  • Chatbots
  • Speech Recognition

2. Word Embeddings

Definition: Word Embeddings are dense numerical vector representations of words that capture their semantic meaning and relationships.

Purpose: They convert Gujarati words into meaningful numerical vectors so that NLP models can understand similarities between words.

Working Principle: Words appearing in similar contexts are assigned similar vector representations in a continuous vector space during model training.

Example: "રાજા" and "રાણી" have similar vector representations because they appear in related contexts.

Advantages:

  • Captures semantic relationships between words.
  • Reduces data dimensionality.
  • Improves prediction accuracy.
  • Represents words efficiently.

Limitations:

  • Assigns only one vector to each word.
  • Cannot understand changing meanings in different contexts.
  • Requires sufficient training data.

Applications:

  • Gujarati Autocomplete & Predictive Text
  • Semantic Search
  • Recommendation Systems
  • Machine Translation

3. Contextual Embeddings

Definition: Contextual Embeddings generate different vector representations for the same word depending on its surrounding context.

Purpose: They help NLP models understand the correct meaning of words based on the complete sentence.

Working Principle: Transformer-based models analyze surrounding words and generate context-aware embeddings for every word in a sentence.

Example: The word "બેંક" represents a financial institution in one sentence and a river bank in another, depending on context.

Advantages:

  • Understands context effectively.
  • Handles ambiguous words.
  • Produces highly accurate predictions.
  • Improves language understanding.

Limitations:

  • Requires high computational resources.
  • Consumes more memory.
  • Training is time-consuming.

Applications:

  • Gujarati Predictive Text
  • Conversational AI
  • Machine Translation
  • Question Answering Systems

4. FastText

Definition: FastText is a word embedding technique that represents words using character-level n-grams instead of complete words.

Purpose: It helps NLP models understand unseen Gujarati words and spelling variations more effectively.

Working Principle: FastText learns embeddings for character n-grams and combines them to generate the final word representation.

Example: "ગુજરાત" and "ગુજરાતી" share common character sequences, enabling better word understanding.

Advantages:

  • Handles unknown words effectively.
  • Works well for regional languages.
  • Improves prediction accuracy.
  • Captures subword information.

Limitations:

  • Consumes more memory.
  • Training is slower than Word2Vec.
  • Requires additional computation.

Applications:

  • Gujarati Autocomplete
  • Spell Checking
  • Regional Language Processing
  • Text Classification

5. Neural Language Models

Definition: Neural Language Models use neural networks to learn patterns in word sequences and predict future words.

Purpose: They estimate the probability of the next word for accurate autocomplete and predictive text generation.

Working Principle: The model analyzes previously typed words and predicts the most probable next word using learned language patterns.

Example: Input: "હું આજે" → Prediction: "કોલેજ".

Advantages:

  • Learns complex language patterns.
  • Provides accurate predictions.
  • Supports contextual understanding.
  • Improves user typing experience.

Limitations:

  • Requires large datasets.
  • Needs high computational power.
  • Training can be time-consuming.

Applications:

  • Autocomplete Systems
  • Predictive Keyboards
  • Chatbots
  • Speech Recognition

6. Transformer-based Representations

Definition: Transformer-based Representations use the self-attention mechanism to understand relationships between all words in a sentence simultaneously.

Purpose: They improve contextual understanding and enable accurate next-word prediction in modern NLP systems.

Working Principle: The transformer assigns attention scores to every word, allowing the model to capture long-range dependencies efficiently.

Example: Input: "હું આજે કોલેજ" → Prediction: "જઈશ".

Advantages:

  • Captures long-range context.
  • Produces highly accurate predictions.
  • Supports parallel processing.
  • Performs well on complex NLP tasks.

Limitations:

  • Requires powerful hardware.
  • Consumes significant memory.
  • Training is computationally expensive.

Applications:

  • Gujarati Predictive Text
  • GPT and BERT Models
  • Machine Translation
  • Text Generation
